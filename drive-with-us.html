Driving activities

Last updated on: October 20, 2024
🖨 🔖 ↗

RTR promotes a wide array of opportunities for members to get out and drive their cars. See below for a summary of driving activities with RTR:

View gallery ⊞

A yellow 992 GT3 RS and red 996 911 driving on track

High Performance Driver Education (HPDE)

These events provide members with the opportunity to develop their driving skills by taking their cars to some premier race tracks with the guidance and instruction of nationally or regionally trained PCA driving instructors.

Autocross

This is a great way for members to enjoy the performance of their vehicles in a safe environment. Autocross events are typically held in large parking facilities with a course created out of a series of traffic cones.

Rally

The objective of a rally is to follow a course, usually on public roads, to the precise specifications prepared by the Rally Master. These events can be competitive, but are typically for fun (and maybe bragging rights).

Touring

The objective of a rally is to follow a course, usually on public roads, to the precise specifications prepared by the Rally Master. These events can be competitive, but are typically for fun (and maybe bragging rights).

PCA Club Racing

Wheel-to-wheel racing on premier race tracks just like you see on TV except that YOU are racing your Porsche against fellow PCA members. These nationally-sanctioned events allow members to use their driving skills honed at Riesentöter Driver Education events or otherwise to race against other Porsches in a fun and generally polite club race.
